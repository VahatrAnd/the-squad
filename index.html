Think Tank Problem Statement

" The problem most people are facing in Rwanda is unemployment. The unemployment rate in Rwanda is higher among the youth population aged 16 years to 30 years (21.5 percent) as compared to one among adults aged 31 years and above (13.1 percent).

Skills mismatch is a discrepancy between the skills possessed by job seekers and the skills demanded by employers can result in unemployment. Job creation occurs in Rwanda when the government invests in infrastructure, creates an enabling environment for businesses to thrive, and promotes entrepreneurship and innovation. Additionally, job creation is also driven by private sector investment and the growth of key industries, such as technology and tourism. This mismatch may arise from gaps in education and training programs or changes in industry demands, Economic fluctuations downturns, or slowdowns can impact job creation and lead to higher unemployment rates, Sector-specific issues Certain sectors may face greater challenges in creating employment opportunities, while others may experience growth and job creation, and Geographic disparities Urban areas may offer more job opportunities compared to rural areas leading to higher unemployment rates in certain regions. "

E-LAB challenges

Challenge #1

Date :

2nd June 2023

Objectif :

Find the exact location and show the proof_ Collect the letters hidden in each location.

Points :

900/1500

Background :

Challenge Number 1 was organized as part of an event at the African Leadership University on the 2nd of June 2023 at 10:00. The challenge aimed to test the participants' skills, resilience, and ability to adapt in a competitive environment.

Key Details :

  1. Quizzes: The clues about the location were presented as quizzes and puzzles that we had to solve in order to know the location. It was very challenging because these were not easy at all, but we still managed to discover the exact locations, which allowed us to get an acceptable grade.
  2. Unfairness: During the challenge, some participants discovered that certain individuals had opened their papers before the designated time. This allowed them to gain knowledge of the exact locations for letters ahead of others. This early access to information created a perception of unfairness among the participants, adding an unexpected hurdle to the challenge.
  3. Physical Demands: The challenge required participants to engage in physical activity, specifically running. The demanding nature of the task made it tiring, but it also added an element of excitement and thrill to the overall experience. Running became an integral part of the challenge, testing the endurance and stamina of the participants.
  4. Solidarity and Communication: Despite the unfair advantage some participants had, the challenge fostered a sense of solidarity among the participants. They formed a bond and supported each other throughout the task, facing the unfairness together. The shared experience of overcoming obstacles and dealing with the challenge's difficulties built a strong sense of camaraderie among the participants.

Moreover, the challenge provided a platform for enhanced communication among the participants. They had to strategize, share information, and work collaboratively to navigate the various obstacles they encountered. This communication aspect allowed them to develop their teamwork skills and fostered an environment of cooperation.

Conclusion :

Challenge Number 1 at the African Leadership University on the 2nd of June 2023 at 10:00 presented a unique set of circumstances. While the early access to information caused a sense of unfairness among the participants, the physical demands of the challenge added excitement to the experience. Most importantly, the challenge served as a catalyst for building solidarity and fostering effective communication among the participants.

The lessons learned from this challenge will contribute to the growth and development of the participants' skills, teamwork abilities, and resilience, which are essential qualities for future endeavors.

Challenge #2

Launching date :

09th June 2023

Objectif :

Making a 30 seconds elevator pitch of each team member telling who they are,their passions , why and how would they solve the problems related to their passion field.

Points :

1000/1000

Key Details :

  1. Everyone had to make a step out of their comfort zone and stand in front of the camera.
  2. The challenge helped each members to get more confident and be used to the pression.
  3. Every failed tentatives made very good bloopers, due to which we have got the maximum grades.

The Challenge at the African Leadership University presented a remarkable opportunity for participants to step out of their comfort zones and face the camera. This seemingly simple task turned out to be transformative, as it allowed each member to grow more confident and resilient under pressure. Embracing the challenge of being in front of the camera, despite the potential for mistakes and bloopers, became a catalyst for personal development and growth.

By pushing their boundaries and embracing vulnerability, the participants discovered the strength within themselves to overcome their fears and insecurities. The experience of facing the camera not only improved their public speaking and presentation skills but also fostered a deeper understanding of their own strengths and weaknesses. Embracing failure as a natural part of the learning process, the team turned their bloopers into valuable lessons and moments of humor, creating a positive and supportive atmosphere.

Through this process of embracing discomfort, the participants learned to navigate through pressure and unexpected challenges with grace and adaptability. They realized that it is okay to make mistakes, and that the most important thing is to learn from them and continue to strive for improvement.

Furthermore, the challenge brought the team members closer together as they bonded over shared experiences and encouraged one another throughout the process. This sense of camaraderie and mutual support became a powerful force that propelled the team to achieve maximum grades.

Conclusion :

In conclusion, the Challenge at the African Leadership University was not just about solving quizzes and collecting letters, but also about personal growth, building confidence, and developing resilience in the face of challenges. Embracing discomfort and taking risks allowed each member to blossom, turning their vulnerabilities into strengths and their bloopers into stepping stones towards success. The lessons learned from this experience will undoubtedly have a lasting impact on the participants, empowering them to face future challenges with newfound courage and determination.
