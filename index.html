Think Tank Problem Statement

"The problem most people are facing in Nigeria is unemployment. The unemployment rate in Nigeria is higher among the youth population aged 16 years to 30 years (23.1 percent) as compared to one among adults aged 31 years and above (14.6 percent).

Skills mismatch is a discrepancy between the skills possessed by job seekers and the skills demanded by employers can result in unemployment. Job creation occurs in Nigeria when the government invests in infrastructure, creates an enabling environment for businesses to thrive, and promotes entrepreneurship and innovation. Additionally, job creation is also driven by private sector investment and the growth of key industries, such as technology and tourism. This mismatch may arise from gaps in education and training programs or changes in industry demands, Economic fluctuations downturns, or slowdowns can impact job creation and lead to higher unemployment rates, Sector-specific issues. Certain sectors may face greater challenges in creating employment opportunities, while others may experience growth and job creation, and Geographic disparities Urban areas may offer more job opportunities compared to rural areas leading to higher unemployment rates in certain regions."

Challenge #1

Date :

2nd June 2023

Objective :

Find the exact location and show the proof_ Collect the letters hidden in each location.

Points :

900/1500

Background :

Challenge Number 1 was organized as part of an event at the African Leadership University on the 2nd of June 2023 at 10:00. The challenge aimed to test the participants' skills, resilience, and ability to adapt in a competitive environment.

Challenge Number 1 at African Leadership University on June 2, 2023, presented a unique set of circumstances. Participants faced early access to information, leading to a sense of unfairness. Physical demands, such as running, added excitement and excitement to the experience. The challenge fostered solidarity and communication, with participants forming bonds and supporting each other. The shared experience of overcoming obstacles and overcoming challenges built camaraderie. This early access to information created a perception of unfairness among the participants, adding an unexpected hurdle to the challenge.

The demanding nature of the task made it tiring, but it also added an element of excitement and thrill to the overall experience. Running became an integral part of the challenge, testing the endurance and stamina of the participants.
  4. Solidarity and Communication: Despite the unfair advantage some participants had, the challenge fostered a sense of solidarity among the participants. They formed a bond and supported each other throughout the task, facing the unfairness together. The shared experience of overcoming obstacles and dealing with the challenge's difficulties built a strong sense of camaraderie among the participants.

Moreover, the challenge provided a platform for enhanced communication among the participants. They had to strategize, share information, and work collaboratively to navigate the various obstacles they encountered. This communication aspect allowed them to develop their teamwork skills and fostered an environment of cooperation.

Conclusion :

Challenge Number 1 at the African Leadership University on the 2nd of June 2023 at 10:00 presented a unique set of circumstances. While the early access to information caused a sense of unfairness among the participants, the physical demands of the challenge added excitement to the experience. Most importantly, the challenge served as a catalyst for building solidarity and fostering effective communication among the participants.

The lessons learned from this challenge will contribute to the growth and development of the participants' skills, teamwork abilities, and resilience, which are essential qualities for future endeavors.

Challenge #3

Launching date :

16th June 2023

Objective :

To tell the untold story of the continent

Points :

1400/1500

Key Details :

  1. Research and Understanding: We researched the continent's history, geography, cultures, and traditions. Understand the significant events, influential figures, and lesser-known narratives that have shaped the continent's identity.
  2. Diverse Perspectives: We recognized that a continent comprises numerous countries, regions, and communities, each with unique experiences and stories. Embrace diversity in your approach, and try to include perspectives from different ethnic groups, socio-economic backgrounds, genders, and age groups.
  3. Unearth Hidden Narratives: We Looked beyond the mainstream historical accounts and uncover lesser-known stories. These could include tales of resilience, resistance, innovation, or cultural achievements overlooked or suppressed over time.
  4. Archival and Primary Sources: Review reputable archival sources, manuscripts, ancient texts, and documents to gather evidence and facts for your storytelling.
  5. Contemporary Perspectives: Understand the continent's challenges, triumphs, and ongoing developments. Include the modern narrative to provide a holistic view of the continent's journey.
  6. Art and Culture: We incorporated the continent's art, music, literature, and other cultural expressions into your storytelling. These creative aspects often convey emotions and historical context that written records may only partially capture.
  7. Balance and Sensitivity: While exploring untold stories, we exercise sensitivity and respect for the cultural and historical contexts.
  8. Collaboration: We engaged with experts, scholars, and locals with deep knowledge of the continent and its stories.

Challenge #3 aims to tell the untold story of the continent, focusing on research, understanding, diverse perspectives, uncovering hidden narratives, utilizing archival and primary sources, contemporary perspectives, and incorporating art and culture. The project aims to provide a holistic view of the continent's journey, incorporating contemporary perspectives, and ensuring sensitivity and respect for cultural and historical contexts. By engaging with experts, scholars, and locals with deep knowledge of the continent, the challenge aims to provide a comprehensive and engaging storytelling experience.

Challenge #4

28th June 2023

Objective :

Make impact.

During the challenge at the African Leadership University, we embarked on a mission to make a positive impact with a limited budget of only $0.5. We decided to spread joy, self-awareness, and motivation to random strangers by purchasing 100 candies and writing heartfelt notes to accompany them. Through this small act of kindness, our goal was to bring joy and positivity to people's lives.

We documented our journey and shared it through a social media campaign using the hashtag #JoyWithCandies to inspire others to do similar acts of kindness in their communities.

Our notes encouraged recipients to participate in small acts of kindness themselves, spreading positivity and self-awareness throughout the community. By leaving candies and notes in public places, we aimed to create surprise and delight, touching the lives of those who encountered them unexpectedly.

Handwriting each note added a personal touch, showcasing our genuine care and effort in brightening someone's day. We included contact information to encourage recipients to share their experiences or connect with us, fostering a sense of community and gratitude.

Through our initiative, we aspired to create a ripple effect of kindness and inspire others to initiate positive change with whatever resources they have.
